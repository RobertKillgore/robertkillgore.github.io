Is Nick Ahmed the best defensive shortshop in baseball?

2021-04-21

Nick Ahmed debuted for the Diamondbacks in 2014, blossoming into a two time Gold Glove winner and becoming the Diamondbacks everyday shortshop. Let’s see if he is the best defensive shortshop in baseball using shortshop fielding data from the last four seasons.

Let’s first look at defensive runs saved, a defensive metric which measures the number of runs a player saved or cost his team on defense relative to an average player. The best fielders typically range from 15-20. On our data set from baseball-reference, it is labeled as Rdrs. First I’m going to only include players who played over 50 games to include the shortened 2020 season. Ahmed’s 2017 season will not be included because he was limited to 47 games played after breaking his wrist midway through the season.

Rdrs <- test %>% filter(G>50) %>% group_by(Name, Season)
ahmed <- Rdrs %>% filter(Name=="Nick Ahmed")

Next, we can create a scatterplot with the eligible players and their defensive runs saved number across the last four seasons

ggplot() + geom_point(data=Rdrs, aes(x=G, y=Rdrs), color="grey") + geom_point(data=ahmed, aes(x=G, y=Rdrs), color="red") +

In 2018 and 2019, Ahmed led the League in Defensive Runs Saved. Not surprisingly, he won his two Gold Gloves in 2018 and 2019. Even in a shortened 2020 season, he was still among the upper half of the league in Defensive Runs Saved. Interestingly enough, Andrelton Simmons actually had the highest DRS in the past four years with a whopping 41 in 2017. Next, let’s look at fielding percentage.

The better fielding percentage a player has, the less errors he commits. To get an idea on where Ahmed’s fielding percentage ranks among the everyday shortstops, let’s look at data from 2018 and 2019. In those two seasons he played over 150 games and played well over 1,000 innings.

eligibleplayers <- test %>% filter(Inn>1000) %>% filter(Season=="2018"| Season== "2019") %>% mutate(Season=factor(Season,levels = c("2018", "2019")))
ahmed <- eligibleplayers %>% filter(Name== "Nick Ahmed")
ggplot() + 
  geom_beeswarm(
    data=eligibleplayers, 
    groupOnX=TRUE, 
    aes(x=Season, y=`Fld%`), color="grey") +

    groupOnX=TRUE,

    panel.grid.minor = element_blank()
  )

Ahmed finished the 2018 season with a .985 fielding percentage in 2018 and a .979 fielding percentage, right among the elite shortstops. The Diamondbacks had the luxury of penciling Ahmed in the lineup everyday, knowing he would make few mistakes.
